How SSS Contributions Work in the Philippines

The Social Security System (SSS) requires members to contribute monthly based on their Monthly Salary Credit (MSC). As of 2025, the contribution rate is 15%, which is shared between employer and employee.

Your contribution determines your eligibility and benefit amount for retirement, sickness, maternity, and other social benefits. Higher contributions generally lead to higher pension payouts in the future.

What is MSC (Monthly Salary Credit)?

MSC is the basis of your SSS contribution. Even if your salary is irregular, SSS assigns a fixed bracket. The minimum MSC is ₱5,000 and the maximum is ₱35,000.
